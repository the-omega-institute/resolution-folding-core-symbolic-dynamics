Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Finite-window rigidity in Fibonacci numeration" for consideration for publication in the Journal of Number Theory.

The paper investigates a natural arithmetic question: given overlapping windows of fixed length m sliding along an arbitrary binary string, when do the local Zeckendorf normal forms--equivalently, the local Fibonacci weighted sums modulo F_{m+2}--determine the original string uniquely? We prove that m >= 3 is the sharp threshold. This local congruence rigidity yields several exact consequences: a bijective block count |L_n(Y_m)| = 2^{n+m-1}, explicit formulas for the distribution of random local Fibonacci residues under Bernoulli input, an exact m-step Markov law for the residue process, and optimal bit-recovery rules from residue windows. The proofs combine elementary properties of Zeckendorf representations with techniques from symbolic dynamics.

We believe this work is well suited to JNT for the following reasons:

1. The central results--local congruence rigidity, fingerprint separation of equi-valued Fibonacci representations, and the exact distribution of random Fibonacci residue vectors--are number-theoretic in both statement and motivation.

2. The sharp threshold m identifies a previously unknown arithmetic boundary for information preservation under finite truncation of Zeckendorf normalization.

3. The paper is entirely self-contained, with all results proved from first principles starting only from Zeckendorf's theorem.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by any other journal. All authors have approved the submission.

Thank you for your time and consideration. We look forward to hearing from you.
